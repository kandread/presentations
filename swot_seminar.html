SWOT satellite concept

The Surface Water Ocean Topography Mission

  • SWOT uses Ka-band radars to measure inundation extent and water surface elevation globally
  • Observations every 10 days (on average)
  • Covers 78°N to 78°S
  • 3.5 year nominal mission lifetime
  • Total budget ~$1.2B

Key takeaways

  • We've been working on SWOT for a long time
  • Preliminary results look very promising for measuring water surface elevation and inundation extent in rivers, lakes, and wetlands
  • Fully understanding SWOT's capabilities is a work in progress
  • Global and local science already happening!
  • Data are publicly available https://podaac.jpl.nasa.gov/SWOT
  • User-friendly platforms available (e.g. Hydrocron)
